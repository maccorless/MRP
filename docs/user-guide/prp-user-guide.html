What is the Press Registration Portal?

The Press Registration Portal (PRP) is the official web application that handles press and photo accreditation for the LA 2028 Olympic and Paralympic Games. It replaces the email-and-spreadsheet workflow of past Games with a single portal that every organisation in the accreditation chain uses.

The portal supports four things end-to-end:

  1. Expression of Interest (EoI) — the public application form where media organisations tell the Games who they are and what accreditation they need.
  2. Review workflow — NOCs and IFs check each applicant, decide who to accept, return or reject, and then allocate a number of press and photo slots per category.
  3. Press by Number (PbN) — NOCs and IFs submit their allocations to OCOG for formal approval.
  4. Governance — OCOG sets review windows and approves allocations, while the IOC oversees quotas, grants Extended Non-Rights Broadcaster (ENR) slots, and manages cross-organisation visibility.
Non-technical promise. If you are an applicant you never need to install anything. Admin users only need a work email and a browser. Every screen has clear actions; every change is logged.

The end-to-end journey

Accreditation happens in five stages. Each stage has a clear owner and hands off to the next. You only need to worry about the stage you own — the portal shows you what is waiting for you and hides the rest.

Who does what

Media applicant Applicant

NOC Admin NOC

National Olympic Committee representative. Reviews applications from their country’s press, allocates slots, submits ENR nominations.

IF Admin IF

IOC Readonly IOC · Read only

Same visibility as an IOC Admin but cannot change anything. Used for observers and auditors.

Signing in

Admin users sign in with email and password at /admin/login. A session lasts 8 hours, after which you are asked to sign in again. Applicants never sign in — they receive a short access code by email when they start, and a 90-day status link after they submit.

Media applicants — apply for accreditation Applicant

If you work for a newspaper, magazine, broadcaster, wire service, online publication, news agency or freelance team and need access to the LA 2028 Games, you will file an Expression of Interest here. The process takes most applicants 20–30 minutes end-to-end.

Step 1 — Start the application

Open the portal and choose Apply as a media member, or go straight to /apply. Enter your work email address (not a personal Gmail or Yahoo account — the system uses your email domain to recognise your organisation).

The first time you use the portal you can read a short explainer of how the process works from the How does this work? link.

Step 2 — Get your access code

After you submit your email, the portal sends you a four-character access code (for example, K7M2). The code is valid for 24 hours and is used only once. Copy it or keep the email open while you fill in the form.

Tip. If you close the tab, you can come back at any time using Check status in the header — the portal will email you a new status link.

Step 3 — Fill in the five-tab form

The Expression of Interest is split into five tabs. You can move between tabs in any order, and the portal saves your progress automatically. Tabs with a green check are complete; tabs with a grey dot still need attention.

Tab 1 — Organisation

Who you are: legal name, type of organisation, website, office address, phone and the email address the accreditation team should use. Only the fields marked with a red asterisk are strictly required, but the stronger your answers the stronger your application.

Tab 2 — Contacts

The primary contact is the person who will receive every update from the NOC and OCOG. Add a secondary contact as a backup — it avoids problems if your primary contact goes on holiday during the review window.

Tab 3 — Accreditation

Tick every category your organisation needs and enter how many slots you are requesting for each. The categories follow the IOC codes (E, Es, Ep, Eps, Et, Ec). If you are a sport-specific outlet, name the sport you primarily cover.

Tab 4 — Publication

Tell the reviewer what you publish: what kind of outlet, how often, how big the audience is, what sports you cover, and where your readers are. This is where the NOC assesses whether your outlet has legitimate reach.

Tab 5 — History

Prior Games coverage (Olympic and Paralympic), examples of past work, and any accessibility needs your team may have for LA 2028. This is your chance to demonstrate you are a returning outlet, or to point to recent work if you are new.

Step 4 — Submit

When every required field is filled and each tab shows a green check, press Submit. You will see a confirmation and an emailed status link that is good for 90 days.

Step 5 — Check your status

Come back at any time through the Check status link in the header. Enter the same email you applied with and the portal emails you a link to see where you are in the process.

Your application moves through these states as the review progresses: Draft Submitted Under Review Candidate Returned Rejected. Only Returned requires action from you — the NOC will tell you exactly what to fix and will re-open your form.

NOC and IF Admins — review and allocate NOC · IF

NOC Admins work through every media application from their country; IF Admins handle their sport’s invited organisations. The two surfaces look almost identical, so this section covers both. Throughout the screenshots we use the USOPC NOC as an example.

Home dashboard

Your home page shows the three things that matter most: how many EoIs still need your review, how much of your press and photo quota you have allocated, and the status of your ENR nominations.

EoI Queue — review applications

The EoI Queue is the main workflow. Each row is one media organisation’s application. Click a row to open the detail drawer on the right.

On each application you can:

  • Accept as Candidate — the application moves to Candidate and is eligible for PbN allocation.
  • Return — send it back to the applicant with a note explaining what is missing.
  • Reject — decline the application; the organisation will see a rejected status.
  • Unapprove / Unreturn / Unreject — reverse any decision while the review window is still open.
Heads up. Once OCOG closes the review window, decisions are frozen. Make sure your EoI Queue shows zero pending rows before the deadline.

Press by Number — allocate slots

After the review window closes for EoIs, move to PbN Allocations. Each candidate organisation gets a row with one number per category (E, Es, Ep, Eps, Et, Ec). The total of each column cannot exceed the quota the IOC has allocated to you.

Offline working. If you prefer Excel, press Export CSV, complete the numbers, and Import CSV back in. The portal replaces your previous allocation wholesale — no merging surprises.

ENR Nominations NOC only

Some media organisations apply as Extended Non-Rights Broadcasters (ENR). These are news broadcasters without a formal Olympic rights deal. They need IOC approval rather than NOC approval, but the NOC proposes a ranked shortlist.

Direct Entry

Sometimes an organisation needs to be on the roster but never filed a public EoI — for example, a long-standing outlet invited directly by the NOC. Use Direct Entry to add them, fill in the minimum required information yourself, and they appear as if they had applied.

Invite Org

The opposite of Direct Entry: generate a pre-filled invitation link and send it to a newsroom so they can complete the application themselves. Helpful for clearing name, country and contact details up front.

Fast-Track

Fast-Track is a shortcut path for applications where the outcome is obvious and you want to move several rows through approval with one action.

Audit Trail & Help

Every change you make is logged in Audit Trail with who, what and when. Use it to answer “when did we return X?” or “who rejected Y?”. The Help tab has a role-specific refresher you can bookmark.

OCOG Admin — approve and publish OCOG · LA28

OCOG is the LA 2028 Organising Committee — the “middle tier” between NOC/IF territory decisions and the IOC’s global oversight. OCOG sets the review windows, approves PbN allocations, handles cross-NOC duplicates, and publishes results to applicants.

Home dashboard

PbN Approvals — sign off territory allocations

The PbN Approvals page lists every NOC and IF that has submitted allocations. For each row you can:

  • Approve — locks the allocation and makes it eligible for ACR.
  • Adjust — edit individual numbers, then approve.
  • Return — send it back to the NOC/IF for another pass.

EoI Summary — pivot view across NOCs

A read-only pivot of every EoI across every NOC. Use it to answer questions like “how many applications are pending across the world?” or “which NOCs haven’t started?”.

Duplicates — cross-NOC duplicate detection

Sometimes a single organisation applies through two different NOCs, or a single journalist appears in two different organisations. The Duplicates panel surfaces suspicious pairs; you can dismiss false matches and escalate real ones.

Master Allocations

A dashboard of the full picture — every NOC, every IF, every category. Use it to spot imbalances before you approve.

EoI Windows — open and close the review cycle

Set the global EoI window open and close date. You can also grant per-NOC overrides — for example, extending a particular NOC by 48 hours.

Publishing results

The Publish Results button on the home page is the moment applicants see their outcome. Before you publish, make sure:

  • Every NOC/IF allocation you intend to use is Approved.
  • ACR has been notified out-of-band (the portal does not email ACR automatically).
  • You are ready for the inbound questions that will follow.
Irreversible in feel, not in code. You can technically unpublish by toggling the switch again, but applicants will have seen their result. Only do it in response to a clear incident.

IOC Admin — govern the process IOC

The IOC surface is where the rules get set. NOCs and OCOG execute inside the boundaries the IOC defines here: quotas, event settings, feature flags, IOC-Direct organisations, and ENR grants.

IOC Overview

The overview page shows every application in the system, grouped by NOC and status. Click any row to see the full detail. The filters at the top let you drill down to specific NOCs or continents.

Master Allocations

The full pivot of every NOC × category, side by side with IOC-set quotas. Over-quota cells are highlighted so you can spot them at a glance.

ENR grants

Each NOC submits a ranked list of ENR applicants. IOC then grants, partial-grants or denies each one, using the combined cross-NOC view.

Quotas

Upload per-NOC / per-IF quota totals via CSV, or edit individual cells directly. These numbers are the caps every NOC sees in their PbN screen.

IOC-Direct

The reserved list — wire services, agencies and other organisations that are not tied to a single NOC. You add and remove organisations here, and you do the NOC-equivalent review and PbN allocation for them yourself.

Org Directory

A cross-NOC directory of every organisation the portal knows about. Useful for looking up an outlet by name, sanity-checking duplicates, or verifying that a newsroom has been correctly assigned to a NOC.

Export

Download the full PbN roster as CSV — the file ACR uses to issue accreditation cards. The export respects whatever state the data is in at export time, so only run it when OCOG has approved everything.

Feature flags

Lets the IOC turn new features on or off without a redeploy. Normal users will never touch this — it exists for safe staged rollouts.

Audit Trail

The master audit log: every write by every admin, across every NOC, IF and OCOG, is visible here. This is the record of truth for compliance questions.

Sudo — troubleshoot as another user

When a NOC reports a problem, you can open a read-only sudo session that shows the portal exactly as that user sees it. Sudo sessions cannot make any changes — every write button is disabled for the duration.

Rule. Sudo sessions are audit-logged and cannot target another IOC Admin.

Glossary

ACR
Accreditation Card Registration — the downstream system that prints and issues physical press cards.
Candidate
An application that has been accepted by a NOC and is eligible for PbN slot allocation.
EoI
Expression of Interest — the public application form submitted by media organisations.
ENR
Extended Non-Rights Broadcaster — a news broadcaster without an Olympic rights deal, granted accreditation by IOC.
IF
International Federation — the governing body of a single sport (e.g., World Athletics).
IOC
International Olympic Committee.
IOC-Direct
Organisations that the IOC accredits directly rather than through a NOC — typically wire services.
NOC
National Olympic Committee — the country-level body (e.g., USOPC for the USA).
OCOG
Organising Committee for the Olympic Games — for LA 2028, this is LA28.
PbN
Press by Number — the numeric allocation of press/photo slots per organisation per category.
Sudo
A read-only session where an IOC Admin views the portal as another user to troubleshoot.

Where to get help

  • Every admin role has a built-in Help tab with a role-specific reference.
  • Applicants can reach back through the Check status link at any time.
  • For unresolved problems, contact your NOC, IF or OCOG liaison — the portal is not a support channel.
  • IOC Admins can use Sudo to investigate user reports directly.
